# Research Prompts for OTA and CIC Award Intelligence

**Purpose:** Detailed research prompts for gathering competitive intelligence on DoD cyber acquisition financial structures.

**Prepared:** November 14, 2025

---

## Prompt 1: OTA and CIC Award Structures (Primary)

```
Research the typical financial structure and award amounts for:

1. DoD Cyber Innovation Challenge (CIC) competitions, specifically CIC #5
2. Other Transaction Agreements (OTAs) under 10 U.S.C. ยง4022 for prototype development
3. USCC (United States Cyber Command) prototype acquisition programs
4. Cyber Fusion Innovation Center (CFIC) funded initiatives

Specifically find:
- Typical prototype contract values ($M range)
- Typical contract duration (months/years)
- Payment structure (lump sum, milestone-based, T&M)
- Follow-on production contract typical values
- Whether prototype winners are guaranteed follow-on funding
- Any public examples of past CIC winners and their contract values

Also search for:
- "CIC #5 Assessment Event" funding details
- PCTE (Persistent Cyber Training Environment) program budget information
- PEO STRI (Program Executive Office Simulation, Training and Instrumentation) acquisition practices
```

## Prompt 2: Historical CIC Winners and Transitions

```
Find information about past Cyber Innovation Challenge (CIC) winners and their outcomes:

1. Which companies won CIC #1, #2, #3, #4?
2. What were their prize amounts?
3. Did they transition to prototype OTAs? If so, what were the contract values?
4. How long was the transition period from CIC award to OTA award?
5. Are there any public case studies of CIC-to-OTA transitions?
6. What was the success rate of CIC winners in securing follow-on funding?

Search for:
- "Cyber Innovation Challenge winners"
- "CIC prototype OTA transition"
- "DoD cyber challenge follow-on contracts"
- CFIC press releases or announcements about past winners
```

## Prompt 6: Milestone-Based Payment Structures

```

3. What is the typical percentage breakdown of payments across milestones?
```

## How to Use These Prompts

### Primary Tool: Perplexity AI
1. Copy each prompt into Perplexity
2. Request citations and sources
3. Ask for specific examples and case studies
4. Request quantitative data where available

### Secondary Tools
- **Google Scholar:** Academic papers on DoD acquisition
- **SAM.gov:** Federal contract awards database
- **CFIC Website:** cyberfic.org for official announcements
- **DoD Budget Justification Documents:** Public budget materials
- **GAO Reports:** Government Accountability Office analyses of OTA programs

### Recommended Search Sequence
1. Start with Prompt 1 (primary overview)
2. Follow with Prompt 4 (PCTE-specific)
3. Use Prompt 5 (statutory authority)
4. Supplement with Prompts 2, 3, 6 (specific details)
5. Use Prompts 7-10 for competitive intelligence

---

## Expected Outcomes

**From these research prompts, you should obtain:**
- Typical prototype OTA values ($2.5M - $10M+ range)
- Typical contract durations (6 months - 3 years)
- Payment structure patterns (milestone-based)
- Historical CIC-to-OTA transition examples
- PCTE program financial profile
- Non-competitive follow-on pathway requirements
- Competitive landscape and other cyber OTA winners

**Use these findings to:**
- Inform Phase 5 negotiation strategy
- Set realistic financial expectations
- Benchmark against competitors
- Prepare resource allocation plans
- Develop milestone structure proposals
